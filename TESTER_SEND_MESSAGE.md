# Tester Send Message (copy-paste)

Paste the block below into a message/email when you send the ZIP. Tweak names as
you like. The ZIP contains everything the tester runs plus two short docs
(`README_TESTER.txt` + `BUILD_NOTES.md`); the feedback questions are built into
the message below, so there's nothing extra they need to open.

---

Hey! I've been making a little game called The World Forgot Us and I'd love your help testing an early version.

It's a short top-down survival demo, about 5–10 minutes, set in a quiet, forgotten wasteland. This isn't the finished game, it's a rough first playable slice, so some art and sound are still placeholder. I mainly want to know if it's understandable and fun to poke at.

How to play it on Windows:

1. Download the ZIP and unzip it somewhere, Desktop is fine.
2. Open the folder and double-click TheWorldForgotUs_Demo.exe.
3. Keep the .exe and .pck file together in the same folder, the game needs both.

Windows might show a blue “Windows protected your PC” warning because the app isn't signed. That's expected because I'm just one person, not a company. Click More info, then Run anyway. If your antivirus complains, let me know.

Controls:
- Move: WASD or arrow keys
- Interact / search / build: E
- Scan: Q or right-click
- Attack: J or left-click
- Pause: Esc

What to do:
Head east along the road, search glowing crates, scan the fallen radio mast to recover a memory echo, then head back west to the base, build the Radio Desk, and rest on the bedroll to save. There's a small teaser at the end hinting at what's next.

What I'm after:
Just tell me honestly:
- Did it launch OK?
- Did you understand what to do?
- Did anything break?
- Was anything confusing, boring, or awkward?
- Did the scanner/memory echo/base part make sense?
- Would you want to see the next area?

Even a couple of sentences back is useful. Bugs and “I had no idea what to do here” moments are especially helpful.

Heads up: it's early. Placeholder characters, no real music yet, and the next area is only a hook for now. Thanks for trying it!
