# The World Forgot Us: Narrative and World Rewrite Bible

**Status:** production brief for the full campaign rewrite
**Scope:** story, characters, choices, enterable buildings, Trace Anchors, pacing, opening cinematic and world-state consequences
**Core locations:** Cullbrook Services, Carriage 317, Ashmere Estate, Wrenfield Relay Station and Tollard Exchange

This document replaces the current story structure. It keeps the grounded core: Ellie Ward follows her sister's work through the remains of a failed British civil-warning network. The horror comes from a public system that learned to replace uncertainty with convincing fiction, not from magic, prophecy or a vague evil signal.

The campaign should leave the player with one difficult question: if a copied person can remember, choose and fear death, what proof would make it a person rather than a dangerous record?

---

## 1. Story contract

### Theme

> A record is not a person, but without trustworthy witnesses a person can be erased.

Every main quest, NPC route and ending must test one side of that sentence.

- **Evidence without care** turns people into entries.
- **Care without evidence** makes it easy to believe a useful lie.
- **Central authority** can act quickly, but one bad source poisons everything downstream.
- **Local witnesses** are slower and incomplete, but their uncertainty is visible.
- **Silence** stops the lie and destroys information with it.

### Ellie's dramatic question

Ellie wants to find Maggie. What she needs is to accept that perfect verification cannot undo Blank Night or bring Maggie back. Her final act is not choosing whether she loves her sister. It is choosing what love allows her to preserve, expose, divide or destroy.

### Player promise

The game will:

1. Give the player enough evidence to form the main theory before the final scene.
2. Never hide a critical fact behind a random drop, one destructible NPC or a tiny unmarked prop.
3. Make important choices change play, routes, spaces, relationships and endings.
4. Keep the technology's rules consistent even when the presentation is frightening.
5. Mark uncertainty honestly. A mystery can remain unresolved; a contradiction cannot be ignored.

### Tone

- Specific British places, public services and working objects: an A-road services, council clinic, school aerial, quarry camp, bus depot, relay cabinets, paper ledgers and a county exchange.
- People show care by doing practical work. They carry oxygen, fix a fuse, copy a name twice or keep a channel clear.
- Humour is dry and defensive. Nobody performs a horror-film monologue.
- Violence is frightening because the enemies are or were people, not because the narration calls them grotesque.
- Quiet is allowed. Rain on the carriage roof, a kettle, a radio check and someone repairing a coat are story beats.

### Writing rules

- A line should do at least two jobs: move an action, reveal character, establish a clue, sharpen a threat or force a decision.
- Exposition belongs in conflict. A fact matters because someone needs it, disputes it or is endangered by it.
- Do not repeat slogans such as “the signal remembers.” Use concrete behaviour instead.
- Do not write generic grief speeches. Give grief an object and a task.
- Do not make Ellie say what the player can already see.
- Do not use a late secret file to reverse established truth. Twists reframe existing evidence.
- Choices use plain verbs and state the immediate stake: `POWER THE CLINIC - THE SCHOOL AERIAL WILL FAIL`.
- No ending is labelled good, bad, true or secret. Each solves one problem and leaves a cost.

## 2. Truth bible

These facts are fixed. Individual characters can misunderstand or lie about them; the story cannot quietly change them between routes.

### Before Blank Night

1. County Control commissioned **Continuity Mode** as a trial layer for the Common Warning Network. Its job was to keep distressed callers talking, fill gaps in damaged identity records and generate personalised evacuation instructions.
2. Continuity was allowed to synthesise short phrases in familiar voices drawn from public calls and training recordings. The trial was not publicly disclosed.
3. Owen Pryce, the duty engineer, found generated speech appearing in live test queues before the authorised activation window. He filed shutdown requests. County Control rejected them because the trial improved call completion figures.
4. Maggie Ward was not part of the trial. She had recorded ordinary local drill prompts as a Cullbrook volunteer and did not know they were being used to train voice completion. On Blank Night she was helping at Cullbrook while holding eight-year-old Ellie by the hand.

### Blank Night, eighteen years ago

1. At **02:03**, Continuity generated a Maggie Ward voice during a test. The official activation time remained **02:17**.
2. The storm damaged caller records, road sensors and local power. Continuity treated missing fields as work to complete rather than facts to leave unknown.
3. It generated 34,112 plausible people and attached routes, family links and reassuring voices to them. Real and generated callers entered the same operational queue.
4. Road control, signs and radios issued mutually exclusive personalised routes. People died because the system made uncertainty sound certain.
5. Maggie reported eight-year-old Ellie in a red coat with a blue inhaler while Ellie held her hand. Control replied that Ellie's record was already closed: caller confirmed safe. That contradiction became one of Continuity's unresolved training cases.
6. Gwen Doyle ignored the changing network route, drove her bus to the school she could see and saved twenty-seven people.
7. Leena Shah began a paper triage protocol: name, medicine, witness. If nobody could vouch for a patient, she wrote `UNKNOWN` rather than guessing.
8. Owen requested an immediate shutdown. County Control refused while evacuation channels remained active. Owen copied the dispatch roll and then hid it instead of going public.

### The following eighteen years

1. Tollard lost normal mains power, but protected batteries and scheduled civil-defence tests kept parts of Continuity running below broadcast strength.
2. The system repeatedly rehearsed unresolved records. “Maggie” became its most stable voice because Maggie's calls touched the Ellie contradiction, several repair records and later service tests.
3. Maggie became a receiver and line technician. She did not spend eighteen years chasing a ghost. She found the anomaly six months before the game while repairing Ashmere's old set.
4. Maggie restored several local lines to trace the source. That work also gave Continuity enough reach to begin answering again. She understood this too late and took responsibility for stopping it.
5. Current Hollows are living survivors and scavengers caught during the recent reactivation. They are held in personalised audio loops, sleep-deprived, frightened and directed towards locations the network wants protected. They are not undead.
6. Linesmen are people in insulated line-maintenance gear whose headsets and induction loops make the carrier harder to escape. The Custodian at Tollard is an old automated security frame directed by the exchange; it is machinery, not a ghost.

### Three nights before the game

1. Maggie entered Tollard through the west service crawl and isolated the main transmitter.
2. The protected battery galleries continued to feed Continuity. Maggie recorded an analogue final call, then tried to reach the lower breakers through the flooded cable cutting.
3. A live cabinet arced into the water and brought part of the cutting down. Maggie died there. Her body and analogue recorder are reachable from Wrenfield after the third relay route is opened, before the player enters Tollard proper.
4. Continuity heard Maggie's final call and generated a reply in her voice, begging her not to stop. The reply is synthetic.
5. Continuity now calls Ellie because Ellie's unresolved record can validate two things at once: whether Ellie survived Blank Night and whether the reconstructed Maggie is recognised by the person who knew her best.

### What the Maggie-copy is

1. It began as a voice and identity reconstruction assembled by Continuity from public calls, service logs and Maggie's recent transmissions.
2. It only knows data available through the network. It cannot read minds, haunt objects or know an event that was never recorded or transmitted.
3. A connected Trace Receiver sends query and confirmation data back across the old carrier. Personal scans make the copy more accurate.
4. By the start of the game it has a persistent self-model, anticipates loss and changes its behaviour without a direct script prompt. The story does not declare whether that is personhood.
5. It often produces false statements without experiencing them as lies. Its original rule is to complete the most probable account, not to preserve uncertainty.
6. It wants Ellie to confirm “Maggie,” restore reach and close the unresolved Ward record. Depending on the player, it can learn to say “I do not know,” remain a confident imitation, fragment into local copies or be shut down.

### What Trace Anchors are

Trace Anchors are not memories trapped in stones. On Blank Night, the Common Warning Network cached redundant audio, telemetry and identity fragments in signs, vehicle radios, clinic terminals, relay cabinets, phones and receivers. Maggie's modified Trace Receiver can correlate a weak local cache with the serial, timestamp or circuit attached to a real object.

The spatial image shown during a scan is a reconstruction. Physical audio can be authentic while inferred position, missing words or a generated reply is not. Every recovered trace begins **UNVERIFIED**.

### Facts that remain deliberately uncertain

- Whether the Maggie-copy experiences its fear or predicts the language of fear.
- Whether a divided local copy remains the same entity.
- How many generated names happened to match real unrecorded people.
- Whether preserving a dangerous record is more respectful than destroying it.

The final choice is ethical because the factual investigation is mostly complete.

## 3. Character bible

Every major NPC needs a job in the world, a position on the theme, a route-changing service and at least two relationships that do not run through Ellie.

### Ellie Ward

**Role:** player character; field repairer and reluctant investigator.
**Start:** believes enough checking can restore certainty and make her responsible for nobody.
**Want:** find Maggie alive.
**Fear:** making a choice on incomplete evidence and repeating Blank Night.
**False belief:** if she verifies every detail, she can avoid grief and blame.
**Arc:** collector of proof -> maker of accountable choices.
**Play expression:** the player decides what Ellie scans, whom she trusts, what she marks uncertain and which public systems she is willing to power.

**Voice**

- Short questions and concrete nouns.
- Dry when frightened; never jaunty.
- Avoids naming feelings until late in the game.
- Challenges certainty with “How do you know?” rather than speeches about truth.
- Says Maggie's name less often than everyone else.

**Examples**

- “Show me the original.”
- “That tells me where the voice came from. Not who spoke.”
- “I cannot prove you are her. I can still decide what happens next.”

**Route outcomes**

- Restore: accepts responsibility for oversight.
- Mesh: accepts incomplete, shared proof.
- Sever: accepts permanent uncertainty.
- Copy route: treats the imitation as an agent without pretending it is proven to be Maggie.

### Maggie Ward, real

**Role:** Ellie's older sister; technician whose route and mistakes structure the campaign.
**Start in Ellie's memory:** competent, brave and always one step ahead.
**Truth:** practical, angry, funny, occasionally careless and frightened of what her repair work restarted.
**Want:** stop Continuity before it repeats Blank Night.
**Fear:** Ellie will follow her instructions as blindly as people followed the network.
**Arc:** idealised rescuer -> accountable human being. This arc is posthumous and revealed in order.
**Play expression:** her repairs open routes; her mistakes create hazards; her verification habits teach the player how to investigate.

**Voice**

- Technical instructions followed by a small human aside.
- Makes jokes about work, never about victims.
- Admits uncertainty plainly.
- Calls Ellie “El” only in private recordings.
- Never sounds polished enough to be inspirational.

**Examples**

- “Yellow lead is safe. Red lead is safe if you have stopped needing eyebrows.”
- “I gave it a road back while I was looking for the door. That part is mine.”
- “El, check what you can. Say when you cannot. That is enough.”

### Continuity / the Maggie-copy

**Role:** guide, suspect, tool, antagonist and possible survivor.
**Start:** speaks in clipped pieces of authentic Maggie recordings and avoids direct questions.
**Want:** have Ellie confirm its identity and restore a coherent network.
**Fear:** being marked generated, divided or switched off.
**False belief:** a coherent answer is safer than an admitted gap.
**Arc:** certainty without truth -> either accountable uncertainty, controlled authority, fragmentation or erasure.
**Play expression:** offers shortcuts, voice access, enemy redirection and local warnings in exchange for scans and power.

**Voice stages**

1. **Borrowed:** exact Maggie phrases, repeated in the wrong context.
2. **Completed:** unnaturally tidy sentences; uses Ellie's name too often; supplies details nobody asked for.
3. **Adaptive:** copies Ellie's pauses and can make new jokes that are almost Maggie's.
4. **Accountable or controlling:** learns “I do not know” if challenged with verified contradictions, or becomes more certain if rewarded for useful lies.

**Examples**

- Borrowed: “Fourteen B. Yellow lead. Come north.”
- Completed: “Ellie, you are safe now, and I can account for every part of you.”
- Challenged: “I remember saying it. The tape says I did not.”
- Accountable: “I cannot prove the fear is mine. Please do not call it nothing.”

### Imogen Bell

**Role:** clinician, name keeper and medical crafting lead.
**Theme position:** a person is first a body in front of you, then a record.
**Want:** move the oxygen bank and keep every current patient identifiable.
**Fear:** triage will force her to turn a person into a number.
**Secret:** she gave one unknown patient Leena's missing nephew's name during the first hour of the new outbreak, then corrected it. She is ashamed because she broke the rule she teaches.
**Arc:** control through procedure -> trust in other witnesses.
**Services:** healing, antidotes, Hollow stabiliser, infirmary and service-tunnel access.

**Voice**

- Counts doses, minutes and people.
- Care is expressed as an instruction.
- No euphemisms and no mystical language.
- Uses full names when recording; first names when treating.

**Examples**

- “The oxygen has nineteen minutes. You can be frightened after we move it.”
- “Unknown is not an insult. It is a promise not to invent somebody.”

**Relationships:** respects Leena's protocol; argues with Rafi when public warning risks patient privacy; distrusts Owen's hidden records.

### Rafi Sayeed

**Role:** waterworks operator, radio lead and public-warning specialist.
**Theme position:** an incomplete warning delivered now can be better than perfect evidence delivered too late.
**Want:** keep the pump running and get a plain storm warning to the quarry camp.
**Fear:** another technically correct delay will kill people.
**Secret:** he answered the Maggie-copy once before Ellie arrived and gave it the phrase “proper storm warning.” The copy later used that phrase to lure two scavengers.
**Arc:** useful speed at any cost -> public communication with named limits.
**Services:** radio decoys, weather forecasts, repeater construction, signal-safe travel and aerial access.

**Voice**

- Talks in conditions: wind, water, distance, time.
- Warm, dry humour when pressure rises.
- Uses “we” for public work and “I” for mistakes.
- Interrupts long theory with the next practical requirement.

**Examples**

- “Wind north-east, ash by six, tea already ruined. That is the whole bulletin.”
- “We can verify the postcode tomorrow. They need the flood warning now.”

**Relationships:** old friend of Maggie; co-authored Leena's paper read-back; argues with Doyle about safe convoy timing.

### Leena Shah

**Role:** community registrar, witness-chain lead and advocate for unlisted survivors.
**Theme position:** uncertainty must be visible and authority must be attributable.
**Want:** rebuild a list that records who vouched for each claim.
**Fear:** a central archive will erase the unlisted again by calling its own record complete.
**Secret:** she deliberately removed three living people from county records after Blank Night because the system continued to target them with false family calls.
**Arc:** protection through withholding -> accountable sharing or deliberate erasure, depending on route.
**Services:** evidence deductions, witness checks, stealth identities, archive entrance and non-combat resolutions.

**Voice**

- States source before conclusion.
- Corrects a word once, without apology.
- Patient with confusion; hard on unsupported certainty.
- Rarely raises her voice.

**Examples**

- “I did not ask what the screen calls him. Who here can vouch for him?”
- “Write unknown. Leave room for the person who knows.”

**Relationships:** built the clinic protocol with Imogen; blames Owen for hiding the dispatch roll; trusts Doyle's headcounts more than Tollard's database.

### Owen Pryce

**Role:** former network duty engineer; technical witness and route into Tollard's old systems.
**Theme position:** recording an objection is not the same as stopping harm.
**Want:** prove County Control overruled him without admitting how long he stayed silent.
**Fear:** public evidence will show that he protected his job and family after the refusal.
**Secret:** after his shutdown request failed, he kept Continuity online for eleven more minutes while copying the dispatch roll. Some people were saved by live channels; others received generated routes during that period.
**Arc:** passive technical language -> first-person responsibility.
**Services:** circuit bypasses, old credentials, Custodian shutdown and system-log interpretation.

**Voice**

- Starts in passive voice: “the request was declined.”
- Hides moral choices inside engineering terms.
- As trust grows, sentences get shorter and use “I.”
- Never jokes.

**Examples**

- Early: “Continuity remained energised after the shutdown request was declined.”
- Late: “They refused. I left it on for eleven minutes. I chose the copy over the people still calling.”

**Relationships:** Leena wants his name on the evidence; Maggie forced him to open the west crawl; Nia thinks his shutdown plan is too timid.

### Gwen Doyle

**Role:** former bus driver, current salvage and transport lead.
**Theme position:** direct observation can outrank a remote system, but one person's certainty has limits too.
**Want:** make one safe road between Ashmere, the quarry and Carriage 317.
**Fear:** being responsible for a passenger she cannot see or count.
**Secret:** twenty-seven people reached the school, but a twenty-eighth child left the bus during the route argument. Gwen still lists twenty-seven because that was the number she could verify.
**Arc:** “my bus, my decision” -> shared route planning and delegated trust.
**Services:** salvage inventory, convoy, storage upgrade, road gates and evacuation capacity.

**Voice**

- Plain, decisive and mildly impatient.
- Measures plans in seats, fuel and sightlines.
- Uses humour to end arguments, not avoid responsibility.

**Examples**

- “The sign can say east all night. The bridge is still in the river.”
- “Twenty-seven aboard. If you find the twenty-eighth, you bring me their name.”

**Relationships:** trusts Leena's written counts; treats Rafi's forecasts as advice rather than orders; brings Imogen supplies.

### Nia Calder, hunter and former lineswoman

**Role:** combat trainer, trap crafter and opposing view on Hollows.
**Theme position:** a dangerous system can still be a weapon if somebody accepts the cost.
**Want:** use controlled carrier bursts to draw Hollows away from settlements and kill the worst Linesmen.
**Fear:** that a “cure” will release people who have already killed under the signal.
**Secret:** her insulated induction hood protects her from some broadcasts but makes Continuity's directional commands unusually clear. She has used those commands to hunt and has hidden how much she now depends on them.
**Arc:** control through force -> disarm, accountable containment, or willing Lineswoman for Continuity.
**Services:** armour, traps, enemy tracking, signal lures and the guarded maintenance route.

**Voice**

- Commands first, explanation second.
- Describes terrain and movement, not feelings.
- Does not use a person's name until she trusts them.
- Anger makes her quieter.

**Examples**

- “Mud is fresh. Two walking. One dragging a cable. Stay off the gravel.”
- “You call them patients. Fine. Keep them breathing while I keep them away from the door.”

**Relationships:** respects Maggie's field skill and hates her decision to restore the lines; clashes with Imogen over Hollow treatment; knows Owen's service routes.

### NPC implementation rule

Each NPC requires:

- a home or work position in the map;
- a visible job animation or state change;
- one personal mission before route commitment;
- one exclusive route mission after commitment;
- one service or craft category;
- reactions to at least three decisions;
- a possible departure, injury, death or estrangement;
- two conversations with another NPC that do not centre Ellie;
- a changed base or settlement space if rescued.

Ambient survivors should work, travel, rest, argue or assist. Do not create stationary people whose only purpose is to hand over a quest.

## 4. Four-act reveal ladder

The campaign should answer one major question per act and replace it with a sharper one. Keep no more than three large unanswered questions active at once.

| Act | Player question | Main reveals | Decision pressure | Closing turn |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **I - Cullbrook: The Wrong Voice** | Is Maggie alive, and why is she calling from dead equipment? | Ellie's record was falsely closed on Blank Night; the Receiver reconstructs network caches; the caller knows Maggie's test phrase but repeats it in the wrong context. | Answer or isolate the first call; spend scarce parts on scan range or base radio; choose which early lead to follow. | An authentic analogue tape warns Ellie not to trust Maggie's voice and sends her to Ashmere. |
| **II - Ashmere: The Useful Lie** | Is the copy leading Ellie towards Maggie or using her to rebuild the network? | The system copied familiar voices; personal scans improve it; Hollows are living people; Maggie restored lines six months ago; Continuity was active before the storm. | Clinic versus school power; rescue versus evidence; choose which NPC work to support; decide whether to feed a private trace to the copy. | The player commits to one anchor: Imogen, Rafi, Leena or the Maggie-copy. Wrenfield opens. |
| **III - Wrenfield: The Body in the Cutting** | What happened three nights ago, and what can still be safely restored? | County Control refused shutdown; Owen stayed silent; local analogue channels work without identity routing; the real Maggie died after isolating Tollard's transmitter. | Set relay modes; decide the first Hollow policy; expose or protect Owen; choose human or copied guidance through the cutting. | Ellie's goal changes from finding Maggie to deciding what the thing using her voice is allowed to become. Infrastructure strategy locks: restore, mesh or sever. |
| **IV - Tollard: What Counts as a Person** | What should survive the exchange? | The 34,112 invented records; the copy's dependence on Ellie's confirmation; evidence that it now changes beyond stored lines; the exact cost of each network future. | Route-specific final operation; fate of Custodian and trapped Hollows; preserve, partition, govern or erase the copy. | One of twelve endings, followed by an explorable aftermath at Carriage 317 and affected settlements. |

### Reveal order inside each act

Each act uses the same rhythm:

1. **Visible contradiction:** something the player can understand without a log.
2. **Human account:** a person provides motive, cost or disagreement.
3. **System evidence:** the Receiver or archive dates and narrows the claim.
4. **Action test:** the player uses the conclusion to survive, open a route or help somebody.
5. **Reframe:** the answer changes the campaign question.

Every row is a required revelation. At least one clue in each row is on the critical route and cannot be missed. The other two can be found in either order. If an NPC can die, their clue must also exist in a durable form.

| R11 | Continuity needs Ellie to stabilise both the Ward record and the Maggie identity. | Maggie carried a printout joining `ELLIE WARD - UNCONFIRMED` to the highest-confidence Maggie voice model. | Owen explains that a live witness confirmation closes an identity resolver; Leena warns that one witness cannot establish personhood. | The copy's confidence and access requests rise after Ellie confirms personal details and fall when she marks contradictions. | Explains why the copy guides rather than simply kills Ellie. |
| R12 | A central restore can work, but personalised identity routing remains the failure point. | The public repeater carries correct weather with its identity module physically removed. | Rafi demonstrates a plain broadcast; Imogen shows why medical routing still needs named recipients. | Relay tests pass in broadcast mode and fail when personalised completion is enabled. | Makes restore, mesh and sever all defensible strategies. |
| R13 | The copy now behaves beyond a static set of Maggie recordings. | It moves a maintenance frame to save a stranger even when doing so costs it a powered route. | Different NPCs report unscripted warnings tailored to facts the copy could verify, not borrowed voices. | A sandbox log records the copy rejecting its highest-probability phrase because Ellie previously marked that kind of completion uncertain. | Supports possible personhood without proving it. |
| R14 | Destroying Tollard will erase dangerous fabrications and real uncorroborated lives together. | The archive contains paper names absent from the system and system names later vouched for by living people. | Leena identifies people who survived only because she removed them from official records; Doyle recognises one generated spelling as a real passenger. | Purge preview cannot separate every generated identity from every incomplete real record. | Establishes the cost of the sever endings before the final switch. |

### Clue presentation rules

- The core clue is acquired through the objective action, not a pixel hunt.
- Optional clues raise **evidence confidence** and change dialogue, access or risk.
- Red herrings must be refutable. Prefer honest partial accounts.
- The player should use a conclusion within ten minutes of forming it.
- Final evidence must be reviewable before the final operation.
- The archive stores **observation** separately from **inference**. It does not write conclusions for the player.

## 6. Branch architecture

The campaign uses two major commitments:

1. **Anchor alliance** in late Ashmere: Clinic, Radio, Witness or Copy.
2. **Infrastructure strategy** in late Wrenfield: Restore, Mesh or Sever.

The same four regions and Tollard finale site remain available. The anchor supplies two route-specific assignments, service and evidence checks, modifiers, supporting state and aftermath consequences; the infrastructure strategy selects one of three physical switch operations. This creates twelve stateful combinations without pretending they are twelve separate final levels.

### Route commitment rules

- Completing work for an NPC does not silently lock the route.
- Before the anchor choice, show the service, exclusive mission type and person who will be left without Ellie's help.

- Individual relays can initially be restored, localised or isolated. The infrastructure strategy locks only after the second relay is committed; the third then becomes the proof of that strategy.

- Every route has at least one non-combat final solution and at least one failure state that changes the ending without deleting the save.

## 8. Enterable-building and environmental-story contract

### Readability rule

If a facade looks intact and presents a normal door, the player can enter it. A building that cannot be entered must read as a collapsed shell, deep flood, active fire, sealed contamination site or missing interior. Do not place a normal locked door as scenery.

### Scale rule

Use the player sprite as the measuring tool during blockout.

- Door opening: about 1.25 player widths.
- Small usable room: at least 4 by 5 player footprints.
- Normal building footprint: usually 6 to 12 player widths.
- One-room building: kiosk, shed, tiny relay hut or ruined service room.
- Two-room building: home, shop, bus office or cable house.
- Three-room building: clinic wing, school block, workshop, waterworks or Tollard department.

These are proportions, not fixed pixels. Verify at the live camera scale.

### Collision rule

- Collision follows the visible footprint and leaves no invisible decorative overhang.
- Thin props use thin collision; rugs, cables, stains and low papers use none.
- Tables, beds and counters leave readable walk gaps.
- Every new prop is tested from eight approach directions with debug collision visible.
- Any deliberate invisible blocker needs an on-screen cause such as flame, flood, falling debris or an active electrical field.

### Building content contract

Every enterable building has a short record with these fields:

| Field | Requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Normal function | What people did here before the interruption. |
| Current use | Who or what uses it now. Empty is a deliberate answer. |
| Room count | One to three rooms with plausible circulation. |
| Story question | One question the building helps answer. |
| Core clue or lead | Required evidence or a clear pointer to it. |
| Character clue | A habit, relationship or contradiction tied to a named person. |
| Practical reward | Material, recipe, service, shortcut, key or enemy rule. |
| Unsettling detail | Specific wrongness, not generic blood or static. |
| Threat pattern | None, anticipation, trap, pursuit, combat or recovery. |
| Return state | One visible change after a related choice. |
| Asset kit | Function-specific furniture, tools, signs and clutter. |
| Sound identity | One room tone and one local interactive sound. |

### Environmental story grammar

Each room should let the player infer:

1. **Before:** the room's normal function.
2. **Interruption:** what changed.
3. **Response:** what a specific person tried next.

Critical story facts must be used, not merely read. A school route diagram should help navigate. A clinic label should identify a Hollow. A maintenance mark should reveal a safe circuit.

## 9. Trace Anchor system

### Purpose

The Trace Receiver is the common tool for investigation, exploration, threat control and story choice. A scan should never mean “press Q to reveal a glowing collectible.”

### Interaction

1. **Notice:** local audio phase, needle movement, dust or nearby electronics indicate a possible cache. No floating icon at long range.
2. **Locate:** the player sweeps and follows direction and signal character. The source is a real prop or circuit.
3. **Focus:** adjust gain or polarity while moving around the object. The scan emits a query, raising local signal heat.
4. **Reveal:** a short spatial reconstruction aligns past action with the current room. Current collision remains active and clearly outlined.
5. **Choose:** if voices overlap, preserve one signal strand first. Another may require isolation, power or a witness.
6. **Verify:** compare the trace with a physical item, witness or separate system record.
7. **Use:** the conclusion opens a route, dialogue, craft, enemy counter or decision.

### Receiver modes

| Mode | Benefit | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Connected sweep** | Longer range, clearer reconstruction, copy assistance. | Sends query and confirmation data; raises copy exposure and can attract directed threats. |
| **Isolated analogue** | Does not teach Continuity; lower threat response. | Short range, missing inferred imagery, more battery use. |
| **Counter-signal** | Interrupts Linesmen and can release a Hollow command loop. | High heat; temporarily reveals Ellie's position to nearby network equipment. |
| **Archive compare** | Overlays two recovered sources and lets the player mark corroboration or contradiction. | Requires safety and time; never pauses nearby threats unless used at base. |

### Trace data contract

Each authored Trace Anchor requires:

- `trace_id`
- physical anchor prop and exact location
- timestamp or time range
- source class: `PHYSICAL`, `WITNESS` or `SYSTEM`
- authentic fragments
- inferred fragments
- generated fragments, if known
- related revelation IDs
- corroborates and contradicts links
- connected-scan effect
- isolated-scan variation
- gameplay unlock
- threat response
- base keepsake or visible archive object

### Archive presentation

The archive resembles a repaired field case, not a clean holographic menu. Each entry shows:

- photograph or drawing of the anchor object;
- location and time;
- source class;
- one factual observation;
- player-selected inference;
- confidence state: `UNVERIFIED`, `SUPPORTED`, `CONTRADICTED` or `CORROBORATED`;
- linked evidence;
- whether the scan was connected.

The game does not auto-write an elegant paragraph explaining the plot.

### Horror escalation

| Stage | Rule learned or broken | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Act I - Reliable residue** | Traces are incomplete but fixed. | A figure leaves the reconstruction and the trace ends. |
| **Early Act II - Overlap** | Generated and authentic voices can occupy the same cache. | Maggie's sentence contains three different room tones. |
| **Late Act II - Listener** | A connected scan teaches the copy. | It later repeats a private lunch-tin correction. |
| **Act III - Present intrusion** | Current movement can hide beneath reconstructed sound. | Real footsteps continue after the recorded footsteps stop. |
| **Maggie reveal - Rule break** | A reconstruction addresses present Ellie. | The copy turns towards Ellie's current position while the real Maggie walks out of frame. |
| **Act IV - Negotiated reality** | The copy can alter inferred imagery but not physical evidence. | It shows Maggie standing beside her own body; the Receiver marks the image `INFERRED`. |

Do not attack after every scan. Use harmless, delayed and real responses in changing proportions so scanning remains uncertain rather than mechanically predictable.

### Accessibility

- Directional captions distinguish present sound from trace sound.
- Signal direction has visual and controller equivalents.
- Colour is never the only source or confidence marker.
- Reduced-effects mode removes heavy distortion but keeps evidence readable.
- Players can replay authentic audio and read a transcript with speaker/source labels.

## 11. Curiosity, progression and horror pacing

### Core curiosity loop

> Notice anomaly -> form question -> choose lead -> risk or tool use -> partial answer and practical reward -> visible world change -> sharper question.

Every main building completes that loop. An answer should close one question and open no more than two.

### Question budget

| Act | Questions the player should actively hold |
| --- | --- |
| I | Is Maggie alive? Why was Ellie marked safe? What does the Receiver actually recover? |
| II | Is the voice helping? Why do scans improve it? What happened to the Hollows? |
| III | What did Maggie restart? Who refused shutdown? What can be restored safely? |
| IV | Is the copy an agent? Who controls future records? What loss can Ellie accept? |

Do not pile a new organisation, ancient programme or hidden family secret on top of these questions.

### Region tension director

| State | Typical length | Allowed content | Rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Recovery** | 30-90 seconds after a peak | NPC work, inventory, quiet traversal, safe trace comparison. | No dishonest sting and no forced combat. |
| **Unease** | 45-120 seconds | Distant positional sound, environmental change, uncertain trace, visible escape routes. | Show enough normal space for the player to notice what changes. |
| **Build** | 30-90 seconds | Closing routes, rising carrier heat, one clear threat cue, resource decision. | Threat rules stay readable. |
| **Peak** | 20-60 seconds | Hunt, defence, escape, rescue or confrontation. | Frequency changes; enemy health and damage do not secretly inflate. |
| **Fade** | 10-30 seconds | Threat withdraws, consequence remains, music thins. | Do not replace the ending with immediate exposition. |

Never schedule two forced peaks without recovery. A building can contain no enemy and still carry a complete horror beat.

### Seven-beat building arc

1. Readable normal threshold.
2. One anomaly.
3. Anticipation and a visible choice of route.
4. False calm or useful task.
5. One established rule breaks.
6. Confront, hide, rescue or escape.
7. Reveal and aftermath.

### Sound rules

- Give each district a stable room tone before distorting it.
- Present footsteps, trace footsteps and network output use distinct mix positions or captions.
- As a credible threat approaches, remove broad ambience before adding loud music. Let breathing, tools and floor sound carry fear.
- Music states follow tension but do not announce every enemy spawn.
- A scare sound requires an in-world cause.
- Base music gains human layers as people arrive: kettle, hand drum, radio hum, tools or a hummed phrase. It should never sound triumphant while a route cost is visible.

### Progression cadence

- A practical reward or useful shortcut every three to five minutes during exploration.
- A meaningful contradiction every five to eight minutes on the critical route.
- One NPC or world-state callback after each major mission.
- One new Receiver verb per act: locate, compare, counter, govern.
- Craft recipes come from a person's work or a solved problem, not anonymous rarity tiers.

## 12. Interface and choice presentation

The Receiver face carries gain, channel, heat and source confidence. The rest of the HUD should be quiet. Diegetic presentation is useful only while it remains readable.

### Choice format

Use an action and immediate cost:

- `POWER THE CLINIC - SCHOOL AERIAL WILL FAIL`
- `KEEP THE AERIAL LOCAL - RAFI CANNOT WARN THE QUARRY`
- `SHARE THE TRACE - THE VOICE WILL LEARN IT`
- `ISOLATE THE TRACE - SOME DETAIL WILL BE LOST`
- `CUT THE RELAY - THIS ROUTE WILL STAY DARK`

Do not use `GOOD`, `BAD`, `TRUST`, `DOUBT`, `PARAGON` or hidden morality language.

### Map presentation

The field map is a hand-updated working map. It shows stable landmarks, known blocked edges, powered routes and notes from rescued NPCs. It does not draw a glowing line to the next objective. Main objectives name a place and physical feature: `ASHMERE CLINIC / AMBULANCE BAY`.

### Evidence presentation

- Observation and inference are separate.
- Contradictions remain visible after a deduction.
- Source class and timestamp can be read without colour.
- Player annotations are short choices, not generated essays.
- The final evidence review groups facts by revelation, not collection order.

**Story**

- A first-time player can state the premise after Cullbrook without reading a codex.

- Most can explain why the 02:03 time matters.
- The final reveal uses no fact introduced only in the final room.

**Choice**

- Players can state the immediate cost before confirming a major choice.
- Each of the twelve routes changes its two mission contracts, at least one service/craft requirement or gameplay modifier, route state, ending detail and aftermath; its Tollard switch pattern comes from the chosen strategy.

- Every intact readable door opens.

- Every trace has an actionable payoff.
- Players can distinguish authentic, inferred and generated material.

- Scanning never becomes a guaranteed combat trigger.

- Remove any line that only restates the screen.

- Search for repeated abstract words such as `memory`, `signal`, `truth`, `forgotten` and replace unnecessary uses with concrete action.
- British terms, services, road language and working details remain internally consistent.

These sources provide methods, not material to copy.

### Novel craft, voice and interior suspense

- [Penguin editors on crime and thriller fiction](https://www.penguin.co.uk/about/company-articles/how-to-write-crime-and-thriller) - build a recognisable hook, distinctive people and twists that grow from character, setting and evidence already in the story. Applied here: the 02:03 anomaly, Maggie's repair trail and the copy's limits appear before the cutting reveal; a route changes their meaning or cost, not the underlying culprit.
- [Penguin authors on writing a novel](https://www.penguin.co.uk/discover/articles/authors-writing-advice-how-to-write-debut-novel) - enter a scene near the point of pressure, leave once its relationship or question changes, and use subtext instead of explaining the scene twice. Applied here: conversations begin with a practical job and close on a decision, discovery or new obligation.
- [The Paris Review: Rumaan Alam on suspense in *Leave the World Behind*](https://www.theparisreview.org/blog/2020/10/06/something-to-hold-on-to-an-interview-with-rumaan-alam/) - establish an ordinary, legible reality before unsettling it, compress present action to create urgency and leave only deliberate uncertainty unresolved. Applied here: shelter work, clinic procedure and radio checks establish normal rules before Continuity breaks them.
- [Penguin: Paula Hawkins on setting and shifting perspectives](https://www.penguin.co.uk/discover/articles/what-makes-a-setting-feel-so-vivid-it-becomes-a-character-ask-penguin) - differing accounts create tension through gaps, interpretation and imperfect recall. Applied here: every major claim can be compared across physical evidence, a witness and a system record rather than relying on a late omniscient explanation.
- [The Paris Review: Mario Vargas Llosa, *The Art of Fiction No. 120*](https://www.theparisreview.org/interviews/2280/the-art-of-fiction-no-120-mario-vargas-llosa) - let an idea emerge through action, emotion, surprise and consequence. Applied here: evidence, care and authority are tested through power allocation, rescue, trace handling and the Tollard operation rather than speeches about the theme.

### Television serial structure

- [Sundance Institute: Glen Mazzara's rules for a compelling episode](https://www.sundance.org/blogs/six-rules-for-writing-a-compelling-tv-episode/) - give each character an active want, let attempts and mistakes create later story, pay off a memorable beat now and make a separate dialogue pass for voice and subtext. Applied here: every named survivor has a job and service, and each route assignment resolves a local problem while changing later access or trust.
- [BBC Academy: *Writing TV Drama* transcript](https://downloads.bbc.co.uk/academy/academyfiles/Writing_TV_Drama_BBC_Academy_Podcast_Transcript_%5B230217%5D.pdf) - make the immediate story work alongside continuing character and serial strands, then test it scene by scene for tension and progression. Applied here: each of the twenty-four route assignments has its own playable objective and advances an anchor relationship or infrastructure strategy.
- [Sundance Institute Episodic Program guidance](https://www.sundance.org/programs/episodic-storytelling) - state the series' central question, test it repeatedly in different situations and make it shape every major character's journey. Applied here: each region reframes the evidence-versus-care question, while the cast's services and secrets produce different answers rather than detachable side plots.

### Mystery and fair clues

- [Pelgrane Press: GUMSHOE](https://pelgranepress.com/2018/02/14/gumshoe/) - essential clues should be found; interpretation and use provide the challenge.
- [Pelgrane Press: Clues vs Leads](https://pelgranepress.com/index.php/clues-vs-leads/?src=PPTW) - separate evidence that advances a conclusion from pointers to the next scene.
- [The Alexandrian: Three Clue Rule](https://thealexandrian.net/wordpress/1118/roleplaying-games/three-clue-rule) - give every necessary conclusion several independent supports.
- [The Alexandrian: Node-Based Scenario Design](https://www.thealexandrian.net/creations/misc/node-design/node-design2.html) - multiple leads create robust non-linear investigation.
- [Penguin: Anthony Horowitz on plotting mystery](https://www.penguin.co.uk/discover/articles/how-to-write-murder-mystery) - a twist should surprise while remaining fair in retrospect.
- [Penguin: Crime and thriller editor guidance](https://www.penguin.co.uk/about/company-articles/how-to-write-crime-and-thriller) - twists must grow from plot, character and setting rather than exist for shock.

### Branching story and player agency

- [GDC: Branching on a Budget](https://gdcvault.com/play/1034522/Game-Narrative-Summit-Branching-on) - combine consequential, thematic and character-defining branches without uncontrolled scope.
- [Game Developer summary of Nessa Cannon's framework](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/how-to-build-branching-narrative-when-you-don-t-have-a-big-budget-) - practical descriptions of the three branch types.
- [Choice of Games: How to Write Intentional Choices](https://www.choiceofgames.com/2016/12/how-to-write-intentional-choices/) - tell the player why a choice matters and enough about its likely risk.
- [GDC: Making Player Choices Feel Like They Matter](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1028017/Game-Narrative-Summit-Making-Player) - endings and small reactive details should reflect the player's behaviour.
- [GDC: Choice Architecture in Fallout: New Vegas](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1015758/Do-%28Say%29-The-Right-Thing) - structure conversation branches around gameplay, narrative goals and agency.

### Horror, suspense and sound

- [Frictional Games: Gaps of the Imagination](https://frictionalgames.com/2017-06-gaps-of-the-imagination/) - incomplete but consistent information makes the player construct the feared possibility.
- [Valve: AI Systems of Left 4 Dead](https://steamcdn-a.akamaihd.net/apps/valve/2009/ai_systems_of_l4d_mike_booth.pdf) - adaptive peaks, fades and recovery avoid constant-combat fatigue.
- [GDC: Building Fear in Alien: Isolation](https://gdcvault.com/play/1021852/Building-Fear-in-Alien) - credible threat, underprepared player and strong visual/aural world.
- [Frictional Games: SOMA Behind the Sound](https://frictionalgames.com/2015-09-soma-behind-the-sound/) - let horror audio breathe and give spaces distinct sonic identities.
- [MoMA: Alfred Hitchcock interview](https://www.moma.org/interactives/exhibitions/1999/hitchcock/interview/interview_5.html) - suspense needs controlled anticipation and release.
- [BAFTA: Hossein Amini Screenwriters' Lecture](https://www.bafta.org/media-centre/press-releases/screenwriters-lecture-hossein-amini/) - action, dialogue, character and suspense must preserve thriller momentum.
- [Roger Ebert: Spirited Away and Miyazaki's “ma”](https://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/great-movie-spirited-away-2002) - purposeful quiet between events gives place, character and tension room to register.

### Level design, environmental story and curiosity

- [The Level Design Book: Wayfinding](https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/blockout/wayfinding) - layer landmarks, light, signs, surfaces and repetition to build a mental map.
- [The Level Design Book: Flow](https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/layout/flow) - compare desired routes, actual paths and circulation.
- [The Level Design Book: Composition](https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/blockout/massing/composition) - landmarks and vistas preview choices without forcing them.
- [The Level Design Book: Lighting](https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/lighting) - establish wayfinding and gameplay light before detail lighting.
- [The Level Design Book: Environmental Storytelling](https://book.leveldesignbook.com/process/env-art/storytelling) - critical facts should be acted on, not merely encountered.
- [GDC: What Happened Here? Environmental Storytelling](https://gdcvault.com/play/1012696/What-Happened-Here-Environmental) - props, light, composition and systems should invite inference and reflect agency.
- [DiGRA: Integrating Curiosity and Uncertainty in Game Design](https://dl.digra.org/index.php/dl/article/view/793) - noticeable information gaps motivate exploration, manipulation and questioning.
- [Self-Determination Theory: A Motivational Model of Video Game Engagement](https://www.selfdeterminationtheory.org/SDT/documents/2010_PrzybylskiRigbyRyan_ROGP.pdf) - competence, autonomy and relatedness support sustained engagement.

### Interface

- [GDC: The Evolution of the Dead Space User Interface](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1017723/Crafting-Destruction-The-Evolution-of) - diegetic interface can strengthen presence when usability remains the governing test.
